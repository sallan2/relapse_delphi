Understanding Relapse in Psychosis

What do People Think?

More About This Study

Relapse prevention is a common goal of evidence based care for people diagnosed with schizophrenia (NICE, 2014). However, within the evidence base that builds clinical guidelines, relapse definitions vary widely across clinical trials of medication (Moncrieff, Crellin, Long, Cooper, & Stockmann, 2019) and non medication research (Cooper, Laxhman, Crellin, Moncrieff, & Priebe, 2019). Furthermore, it is less clear what people with a diagnosis of schizophrenia and ther supporters might think is important to include within a definition of relapse. Using a Delphi study, this research aims to recruit researchers working in research looking at relapse, people with a diagnosis of schizophrenia and their supporters to identify and attempt to reach agreement on what factors are important to include in a definition of relapse.
